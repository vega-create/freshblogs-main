Owning a home means something will always need fixing, adjusting, or improving. Having the right tools on hand when those moments arrive saves you time, money, and the frustration of improvising with whatever happens to be in the kitchen drawer. This guide covers the 25 tools every homeowner should own, organized by category so you can build your collection strategically.

You do not need to buy everything at once. Start with the essentials and add specialty tools as your projects demand them. Every tool listed here includes what it does, what features to look for when shopping, and a realistic price range.

## Hand Tools (10 Essentials)

### 1. Claw Hammer (16 oz)

A 16-ounce claw hammer is the right weight for most household tasks. It drives nails, pulls them out with the claw end, and provides light demolition power when needed.

**What to look for:** A fiberglass or steel handle absorbs vibration better than wood. A smooth face is more forgiving than a milled face for general use. Ergonomic rubber grips reduce hand fatigue.

**Price range:** $10-$30

### 2. Screwdriver Set

You need both Phillips (cross-head) and flathead (slotted) screwdrivers in multiple sizes. A good set includes at least three sizes of each type.

**What to look for:** Magnetic tips hold screws in place while you work. Hardened steel tips resist stripping. A comfortable, non-slip handle gives you better torque. Some sets include square (Robertson) and star (Torx) drivers, which are increasingly common in modern hardware.

**Price range:** $15-$40 for a set of 6-10

### 3. Pliers (3 types)

Three types of pliers cover most situations: needle-nose for precision work and tight spaces, slip-joint (standard) for general gripping, and tongue-and-groove (Channellock-style) for plumbing and larger fasteners.

**What to look for:** Spring-loaded handles reduce hand fatigue. Machined jaws grip better than stamped jaws. Insulated handles are a bonus for any work near wiring.

**Price range:** $20-$50 for a 3-piece set

### 4. Tape Measure (25 ft)

A 25-foot tape measure handles any room in a typical house and is long enough to measure exterior walls.

**What to look for:** A 1-inch-wide blade stays rigid when extended, preventing sag. A magnetic hook grabs metal studs and pipes. A standout distance of at least 7 feet lets you measure across a room without a helper.

**Price range:** $10-$25

### 5. Level (Torpedo and 24-inch)

A torpedo level (9 inches) fits in tight spaces and on pipes, while a 24-inch level handles shelves, cabinets, and picture frames.

**What to look for:** Acrylic vials are more durable than glass. Machined aluminum bodies are straight and sturdy. Some models include magnetic edges for hands-free use on metal surfaces.

**Price range:** $10-$20 each

### 6. Utility Knife

A retractable utility knife handles everything from opening boxes to trimming caulk, scoring drywall, and cutting carpet.

**What to look for:** A quick-change blade mechanism lets you swap blades without tools. A sturdy locking mechanism prevents the blade from retracting under pressure. Buy extra blades in bulk since a dull blade is both ineffective and dangerous.

**Price range:** $8-$20

### 7. Adjustable Wrench Set

Two adjustable wrenches, one 8-inch and one 12-inch, handle most nuts and bolts around the house. They are essential for plumbing connections, furniture assembly, and appliance installation.

**What to look for:** A wide jaw opening accommodates larger hardware. A slim profile fits into tight spaces. Laser-etched measurement markings on the jaw help you identify fastener sizes.

**Price range:** $15-$35 for a 2-piece set

### 8. Pry Bar (Flat Bar)

A flat pry bar removes trim, pulls nails, scrapes adhesive, and separates boards. The flat profile slips behind molding without damaging the wall.

**What to look for:** A thin, flat profile with a beveled edge slides behind trim easily. Dual ends give you different leverage options. Forged steel resists bending.

**Price range:** $8-$15

### 9. Allen Key Set (Hex Keys)

Allen keys assemble and tighten furniture, tighten set screws on faucet handles, and adjust door hinges. Both metric and SAE (standard) sets are necessary since you will encounter both systems.

**What to look for:** Ball-end keys allow you to insert at an angle for tight spots. Color-coded sets help you quickly distinguish metric from SAE. A folding set keeps everything organized.

**Price range:** $8-$20 for both sets

### 10. Clamps (2-4 pieces)

Clamps hold pieces together while glue dries, keep workpieces steady while you cut or drill, and serve as an extra set of hands when you are working alone.

**What to look for:** Quick-release trigger clamps are faster and easier to use than screw clamps. A 6-inch clamp size handles most household tasks. Rubber-padded jaws protect surfaces from marring.

**Price range:** $5-$15 each

## Power Tools (8 Essentials)

### 11. Cordless Drill/Driver

The most versatile power tool you can own. It drills holes, drives screws, and accepts dozens of accessories for specialized tasks.

**What to look for:** A 20-volt lithium-ion battery provides plenty of power for household tasks. Two speed settings give you control for delicate and heavy-duty work. A brushless motor lasts longer and runs cooler. Buy a kit with two batteries so one charges while you use the other.

**Price range:** $60-$150

### 12. Circular Saw

A circular saw cuts lumber, plywood, and sheet goods quickly and accurately. It handles framing, shelving, and any project requiring straight cuts in wood.

**What to look for:** A 7 1/4-inch blade is the standard size that cuts through 2x lumber at any angle. A laser guide or LED shadow line improves cut accuracy. An electric brake stops the blade quickly when you release the trigger.

**Price range:** $50-$120

### 13. Jigsaw

A jigsaw cuts curves, circles, and irregular shapes that a circular saw cannot. It is essential for cutting holes in countertops, trimming door casings, and craft projects.

**What to look for:** Orbital action settings let you adjust for speed versus cut smoothness. Tool-free blade changes speed up the process. A variable speed trigger gives you more control on delicate materials.

**Price range:** $40-$100

### 14. Random Orbital Sander

An orbital sander smooths wood surfaces for painting or staining far faster than hand sanding. The random orbital pattern prevents the swirl marks that sheet sanders leave behind.

**What to look for:** 5-inch pads are the standard size with widely available sandpaper discs. A dust collection port connects to a shop vacuum to keep your workspace clean. Variable speed lets you match the speed to the material.

**Price range:** $40-$80

### 15. Impact Driver

An impact driver delivers high rotational torque for driving long screws, lag bolts, and fasteners that would stall a standard drill. It is invaluable for deck building, framing, and any project with heavy fasteners.

**What to look for:** Many brands offer drill/impact driver combo kits that share batteries. A 1/4-inch hex chuck accepts all standard driver bits. Three speed settings prevent overdrive on delicate materials.

**Price range:** $50-$100 (or $100-$180 for a combo kit with drill)

### 16. Oscillating Multi-Tool

An oscillating multi-tool sands in corners, cuts flush against surfaces, removes grout, and trims door casings. The oscillating blade action reaches places no other tool can.

**What to look for:** Tool-free blade changes save time. A wide range of compatible accessories increases versatility. Variable speed control helps you match the tool to the task.

**Price range:** $40-$100

### 17. Stud Finder

An electronic stud finder locates the wood framing behind your walls so you can anchor shelves, TVs, and heavy items securely. Advanced models also detect electrical wiring and plumbing.

**What to look for:** A deep scan mode finds studs through thicker walls. Wire detection warns you before drilling into live electrical lines. An edge-detection model marks both edges of the stud for precise centering.

**Price range:** $20-$50

### 18. Shop Vacuum

A shop vacuum handles sawdust, drywall dust, water spills, and debris that would destroy a household vacuum. It connects to power tools with dust ports to keep your workspace clean.

**What to look for:** A 4-6 gallon capacity is adequate for most home projects. A blower function reverses airflow to clear leaves and debris. HEPA filtration captures fine dust particles. Wet/dry capability handles flooded basements and water heater leaks.

**Price range:** $50-$120

### 20. Combination Square

A combination square marks 90-degree and 45-degree angles with precision. It also measures depth, checks edges for squareness, and scribes parallel lines.

**What to look for:** A hardened steel blade with clear etched markings. A cast metal head rather than plastic, which wears quickly. A locking mechanism that holds the blade firmly at any extension.

**Price range:** $10-$25

### 21. Laser Level

A laser level projects perfectly straight horizontal and vertical lines on walls, making it invaluable for hanging pictures, installing shelves, aligning tiles, and setting cabinets. It replaces the old method of measuring, marking, and snapping chalk lines.

**What to look for:** Self-leveling capability saves setup time. Both horizontal and vertical line projection. A mounting bracket or tripod thread for hands-free operation. Green lasers are easier to see than red in bright rooms.

**Price range:** $25-$80

## Safety Equipment (3 Essentials)

### 23. Safety Goggles

Eye protection is non-negotiable for any task involving cutting, drilling, sanding, or working overhead. Flying debris, sawdust, and chemical splashes can cause permanent eye damage.

**What to look for:** Anti-fog coating keeps lenses clear during physical work. A snug seal around the edges prevents particles from entering from the sides. Over-glasses models fit over prescription eyewear.

**Price range:** $8-$20

### 24. Work Gloves

Work gloves protect your hands from splinters, sharp edges, hot surfaces, and abrasions. Different tasks call for different gloves.

**What to look for:** Leather or synthetic leather gloves for general work, rubber-coated gloves for grip, and cut-resistant gloves for utility knife work. Touchscreen-compatible fingertips let you check your phone without removing them.

**Price range:** $10-$25

### 25. Hearing Protection

Power tools, especially circular saws and impact drivers, produce noise levels that can cause permanent hearing damage with prolonged exposure. Earplugs or over-ear muffs are essential.

**What to look for:** A noise reduction rating (NRR) of 22 or higher for power tool use. Over-ear muffs are easier to put on and take off repeatedly. Electronic muffs amplify conversation while blocking harmful noise, which is useful when working with a helper.

**Price range:** $10-$30

## Building Your Kit: A Practical Approach

Rather than buying everything at once, build your collection project by project. When you decide to paint a room, buy the hammer, screwdriver set, tape measure, and utility knife you need for prep work. When you install shelves, add the drill and level. Each project naturally expands your toolkit.

Store your tools in a dry location. A basic toolbox or tool bag keeps hand tools organized, while a wall-mounted pegboard in a garage or closet provides easy access and visibility. Moisture is the enemy of metal tools, so avoid damp basements and add a packet of silica gel to your toolbox if humidity is a concern.

Quality hand tools from reputable brands often come with lifetime warranties. If a wrench breaks or a screwdriver tip strips, the manufacturer will replace it. This makes investing in quality hand tools a genuinely long-term purchase.
